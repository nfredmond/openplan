# OpenPlan Pilot Onboarding Checklist — 2026-04-07

**Owner:** Owen Park  
**Support:** Bartholomew Hale, Mateo Ruiz  
**Status:** active internal checklist

## Purpose

Provide a repeatable onboarding checklist for supervised OpenPlan pilots.

## Pre-kickoff

- Confirm agency name, project context, and pilot contact.
- Confirm pilot owner on the Nat Ford side.
- Confirm the first workflow to prove.
- Confirm the success criteria in plain language.
- Confirm whether the pilot is using public pricing baseline or a custom supervised wrapper.
- Confirm billing language will remain evidence-accurate.

## Workspace readiness

- Create or select the correct workspace.
- Confirm workspace owner/admin access.
- Confirm the billing workspace context is the intended one.
- Confirm the pilot workspace name is human-readable and not confusing.
- Confirm no stale QA/test workspace is being used by mistake.

## Pilot scope lock

- Record the exact workflow(s) in scope.
- Record what is explicitly out of scope.
- Confirm no validated forecasting claim is being implied.
- Confirm no compliance/legal automation claim is being implied.
- Confirm support boundaries and response expectations.

## Kickoff session

- Walk the pilot contact through the workspace and workflow.
- Confirm the first data/context inputs.
- Confirm the success criteria again live.
- Note any assumptions or missing prerequisites.
- Stop immediately if context, billing, or ownership looks wrong.

## First workflow completion

- Complete the first guided workflow end to end.
- Capture what was manual vs automated.
- Confirm outputs are understandable to the pilot contact.
- Record any blocker, workaround, or limitation.
- Decide whether the midpoint review should stay on the same workflow or expand scope.

## Midpoint review

- Confirm whether the pilot is meeting the success criteria.
- Note what has worked well.
- Note what remains bounded or manual.
- Confirm whether any new request should be deferred to a later phase.

## Closeout prep

- Summarize what was piloted.
- Summarize what worked.
- Summarize what remained bounded.
- Summarize what support was required.
- Summarize the recommended next step: expand, revise, or stop.

## Stop conditions

Pause the pilot if any of the following occur:
- wrong workspace or agency context,
- out-of-scope modeling/compliance claims,
- billing ambiguity,
- missing reviewer/approval requirement,
- unpaid custom-work drift.

## Bottom line

A supervised pilot should feel controlled, truthful, and useful, not improvised.

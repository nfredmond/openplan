## Purpose
This packet defines the truthful, supervised external pilot posture for OpenPlan.

It is meant to support a real pilot conversation without overclaiming product maturity, billing automation, modeling validity, or compliance completeness.

## What this pilot is
A supervised pilot is a human-led, scoped engagement in which OpenPlan is used against a named agency or project context with clear boundaries, a named support path, and a documented closeout.

The pilot is intended to demonstrate that OpenPlan can support a real planning workflow, not to pretend the product is already a universally self-serve SaaS platform.

## What OpenPlan can honestly pilot today
Depending on the pilot scope, OpenPlan can support one or more of the following proven lanes:

- authenticated planning workspace continuity,
- projects / plans / programs / models / reports surfaces,
- managed run launch and linked analysis history,
- scenario comparison,
- county-run onboarding and scaffold editing,
- engagement-to-report handoff and traceability,
- guided corridor / analysis workflows where the pilot scope is explicitly bounded.

## What is in scope
A supervised pilot may include:

1. **Kickoff and scope confirmation**
   - confirm the agency or project context,
   - agree the pilot objective,
   - name the primary workspace and pilot owner,
   - define success criteria in plain language.

2. **Workspace onboarding**
   - create or select the correct workspace,
   - verify the intended billing/workspace context,
   - confirm who can review or approve the pilot.

3. **One or more guided workflows**
   - project/plan/model/program continuity,
   - managed run launch,
   - scenario comparison,
   - county-run scaffold or equivalent planning-control workflow,
   - report traceability / source navigation,
   - corridor analysis if that is the selected pilot use case.

4. **Closeout**
   - review what worked,
   - note what remains bounded or manual,
   - decide whether the next step is expansion, revision, or a follow-on contract.

## What is out of scope
The pilot does **not** include:

- a promise of validated forecasting,
- a claim of calibrated or behavioral-demand readiness,
- full LAPM / legal-grade compliance automation,
- a guarantee of self-serve onboarding with no human assistance,
- 24/7 support,
- unlimited custom modeling work,
- unrestricted change orders,
- or any claim that the product is already a mature enterprise procurement system.

## Pilot onboarding
### Onboarding sequence
1. Confirm the correct organization and pilot contact.
2. Confirm the intended workspace or bootstrap a new one.
3. Select the first workflow to prove.
4. Run the kickoff call and document the success criteria.
5. Load the initial data or select the existing production-backed surface to exercise.
6. Complete the first workflow together.
7. Schedule midpoint and closeout reviews.

### Expected operator behavior
- The pilot is human-led, not autonomous.
- The pilot owner stays available during critical steps.
- Any context mismatch is treated as a stop condition, not something to paper over.

## Support path
Support during a supervised pilot is intentionally bounded and human-led.

### Named roles
- **Primary ops owner:** COO / operations lead
- **Governance reviewer:** Principal Planner
- **Executive sponsor:** CEO

### Support rules
- Kickoff, midpoint, and closeout calls are part of the standard pilot structure.
- Email support target: acknowledge within 2 business days during the pilot window.
- If a blocker is material, escalate to the named ops owner instead of guessing.
- If a review or approval is required, the pilot pauses until the review is complete.
- There is no promise of 24/7 response or always-on concierge support.

## AI disclosure
AI is used in OpenPlan as an accelerator, not as an unreviewed authority.

### Required disclosure
- AI may help draft, summarize, and organize content.
- Final planning interpretation, external wording, and deliverable approval remain human responsibilities.
- No AI-generated output should be treated as final without qualified human review.

### What AI is not
- not legal advice,
- not a compliance substitute,
- not a modeling validation authority,
- not a replacement for principal review,
- not a reason to skip evidence checks.

## Pilot success criteria
A pilot is successful when the agency can say, without hand-waving, that:

- the chosen workflow was completed end to end,
- the outputs were understandable and useful,
- the support path felt clear and accountable,
- the product did not overpromise,
- and the next step is obvious.

Success may mean expansion, a deeper contract, or a narrower use case. It does not have to mean full product adoption on day one.

## Stop conditions
Pause the pilot immediately if any of the following occur:

- the workspace or organization context is wrong,
- the pilot asks for a capability that is not in scope,
- the product starts implying validated forecasting or compliance maturity it does not have,
- the billing path becomes ambiguous,
- a required review is pending,
- or the pilot begins drifting into unpaid custom work.

## Suggested client-safe phrasing
Use this wording when you need a concise description:

> OpenPlan is a production-backed planning operating system for supervised pilot use. It is strongest today in authenticated planning continuity, report traceability, scenario coordination, and guided county or analysis workflows. We support pilots with human-led onboarding, explicit scope, and evidence-accurate claims.

## Suggested closeout output
Each pilot should end with a short closeout note covering:

- what was piloted,
- what worked,
- what remained bounded,
- what support was required,
- what the billing posture was,
- and whether the next step is expansion, revision, or stop.

A supervised pilot is the right external motion for OpenPlan right now.

It is honest, useful, and consistent with the current evidence boundary.

# OpenPlan Pricing & Positioning Decision Note — April 2026

**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Owner:** Bartholomew Hale (COO)
**Status:** Canonical pricing / positioning decision note

## Decision
Use the live website pricing page as the canonical public pricing reference:

- **Starter:** $249/mo
- **Professional:** $799/mo

Use the supervised-pilot and service-wrapper language for custom or negotiated engagements.

Do **not** keep quoting the older $599 / $1,499 monthly ladder as though it is the current public truth.

## Why this decision was needed
The repo still contains two pricing stories:

1. the current public pricing page, which shows a pilot-era baseline of $249 / $799, and
2. older sales collateral that still references a different monthly ladder and a $1,500 fixed-fee pilot credit structure.

Both may be historically explainable. They are not equally canonical.

If we keep both stories live without a decision, we create drift in sales, support, onboarding, and public-facing copy.

## Current source of truth
### Canonical public pricing
The public pricing page is the current customer-facing baseline and should be treated as the truth for:

- the website,
- quick sales replies,
- pilot conversion conversations,
- and any summary that needs one current number set.

### Historical / negotiated-commercial references
The following can still exist as historical or negotiated-commercial references, but should not be quoted as the current website truth unless explicitly revised:

- `docs/sales/2026-02-24-openplan-pilot-conversion-kit.md`
- `docs/sales/2026-02-24-openplan-pilot-sow-one-pager.md`

Those docs were useful in the earlier pilot framing, but they now need either revision or a clear archival label if reused.

## Positioning decision
Use this position:

> OpenPlan is a production-backed planning operating system for supervised pilot use. It is strongest today in authenticated planning continuity, report traceability, scenario coordination, and guided county or analysis workflows.

This is the right positioning because it matches current proof without overreaching into claims we do not yet have.

## What to avoid saying
Do not position OpenPlan as:

- a fully launched self-serve municipal SaaS,
- a validated forecasting platform,
- a finished compliance automation platform,
- or a mature enterprise procurement system.

Do not say the product is “free forever” or “open-source corridor analysis” as the primary commercial story. That is historical context, not current launch truth.

## Pricing / packaging guidance
### For public-facing materials
Use the current website pricing numbers.

### For supervised pilots
If the engagement is a guided design-partner pilot or custom service wrapper, be explicit that:

- the scope is negotiated,
- the support path is human-led,
- the billing path may be invoice-assisted or contract-assisted,
- and the public pricing page is the baseline reference, not a universal promise.

### For fixed-fee pilot language
A fixed-fee pilot can still exist, but only if it is framed as a negotiated service offer and not as a second canonical price sheet.

If used, it should be written as:
- a scoped pilot fee,
- a scoped support package,
- or a custom pilot SOW.

It should not coexist in a sales reply as a conflicting “other official price” unless that conflict is deliberately explained.

## Risks if we do nothing
- Sales replies will keep quoting inconsistent numbers.
- Pilot buyers will compare one doc against another and lose trust.
- Support will inherit ambiguous expectations about what is included.
- The public website, pilot packet, and sales collateral will disagree.

That is exactly how “minor wording differences” become expensive.

## Risks of the chosen decision
The live website pricing may be lower than some earlier commercial instincts.
That creates two real risks:

1. **Underpricing support burden.**
   The $249 / $799 structure can work only if support and onboarding expectations stay bounded.

2. **Confusing pilots with self-serve subscriptions.**
   If the team promises too much support inside a low-friction monthly plan, the economics get sloppy very quickly.

The mitigation is simple: keep the scope narrow, keep support human-led, and use custom pilot wrappers when the work is not commodity self-serve.

## Recommended operating rules
1. Quote the live public pricing page as the default public truth.
2. Treat older pricing collateral as historical unless revised.
3. Use a separate supervised-pilot/service-wrapper sentence when the contract is custom.
4. Never mix historical and current pricing in one outward-facing answer without explicitly calling out the difference.
5. If someone asks for the “real” price, answer with one clean current number set and one clear caveat about pilot scope.

## Suggested internal wording
> The canonical public pricing is the current website ladder: Starter at $249/mo and Professional at $799/mo. Older $599 / $1,499 collateral is historical and should not be treated as the current public price sheet. Any custom pilot can still be quoted as a separate supervised service wrapper.

## Bottom line
The pricing decision is now simple:

- **Public truth:** $249 / $799
- **Custom pilot truth:** negotiated supervised wrapper
- **Historical collateral:** archive or revise

That keeps the market story clean and stops the repository from arguing with itself.

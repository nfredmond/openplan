## Executive positioning
OpenPlan is now a credible, production-backed planning operating system for early pilot use.

It is strongest today in:
- project-centered planning workflows,
- authenticated multi-module continuity across core planning surfaces,
- structured report generation,
- engagement-to-report handoff, and
- auditable report traceability back to engagement source context.

The correct external posture is **confident but disciplined**:
- say it is production-backed and pilot-ready for scoped use,
- do not say every commercial proof question was freshly re-run this cycle,
- do not imply a broader proof record than the evidence supports.

## Approved talking points
Use these when speaking plainly:
- OpenPlan is **production-backed** for the current planning-domain workflow core.
- OpenPlan is **pilot-ready for scoped use**.
- OpenPlan emphasizes **auditable workflow continuity** rather than black-box automation.
- OpenPlan now supports **engagement-to-report traceability** on the live product.
- OpenPlan is being positioned with **evidence-first language**, not marketing exaggeration.

## Phrases to avoid
Do **not** say:
- “fully proven in every respect”
- “commercially closed in every lane”
- “all billing/payment questions were freshly re-proven this cycle”
- “ready for any agency in any context without qualification”
- “infallible,” “fully automated,” or similar absolute claims

## Safer substitutes
Prefer:
- **production-backed**
- **pilot-ready for scoped use**
- **auditable**
- **quality-controlled**
- **human-reviewed at critical gates**
- **evidence-based**
- **designed for operational clarity and traceability**

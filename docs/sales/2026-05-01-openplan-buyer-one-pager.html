External briefing

The offer

OpenPlan is a supervised planning workbench where projects, plans, funding, maps, model runs, public comments, reports, and evidence stay connected. The near-term sale is implementation and managed service support around scoped planning workflows.

Proof boundary

OpenPlan is ready for supervised pilots and managed-service engagements where the workflow is scoped, evidence-backed, and human-reviewed.

Connected planning spine Records to evidence to deliverables
Workspace records projects, plans, programs
Evidence maps, data, comments, runs
Workflows RTP, grants, reports
Services hosting, onboarding, support

Who it is for

  • Rural RTPAs, counties, and small transportation commissions.
  • Cities and towns managing plans, grants, project lists, and maps.
  • Tribes needing LRTP, TTIP/TIP, safety, grant, and infrastructure evidence support.
  • Consultants managing multiple planning clients and defensible deliverables.
  • MPOs or state/regional agencies coordinating programs and reporting.

What OpenPlan helps with today

  • Shared project-spine proof across workflows.
  • RTP and report packet workflow proof.
  • Grants, funding, reimbursement, and closeout posture.
  • Engagement intake, moderation, and report handoff.
  • Analysis and model-run output linked into reports.
  • Admin/support intake, reviewer triage, provisioning, and owner invitations.

Why it is different

  1. Planning concepts are native: RTPs, grants, comments, project lists, funding cycles, model runs, and board packets.
  2. Evidence is connected across records, maps, datasets, comments, scenarios, reports, and exports.
  3. The core is open source, so agencies can inspect the code and avoid closed vendor dependency.
  4. The posture is rural, tribal, and small-agency friendly; it does not assume a large internal GIS or modeling shop.
  5. AI is supervised assistance. Human review and evidence remain central.
  6. The goal is deliverables, not only dashboard views.

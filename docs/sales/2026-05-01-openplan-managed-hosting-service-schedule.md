# OpenPlan Managed-Hosting Service Schedule

_Date: 2026-05-01_
_Status: Buyer-safe service schedule template; legal/procurement review required before signature._

## Schedule Purpose

This schedule converts the managed-hosting service description into buyer-reviewable operating terms for a scoped OpenPlan engagement. It is intended to attach to a pilot SOW, order form, or services agreement.

This schedule does not replace legal terms, insurance terms, data-processing terms, procurement terms, or agency counsel review. It defines the operational service posture Nat Ford can scope around the Apache-2.0 OpenPlan core.

## Service Summary

Nat Ford operates one or more hosted OpenPlan workspaces for the client so the client can use OpenPlan as a supervised planning workbench without self-hosting the application.

The hosted service is a managed-service wrapper around the open-source core. It is not a proprietary software license, not a broad self-serve municipal SaaS claim, and not a replacement for planner, engineer, legal, compliance, or agency review.

## Included Baseline Services

- Hosted OpenPlan workspace operation for the scoped workspace(s).
- Workspace activation, owner invitation, and basic user/access coordination.
- Service-lane intake and setup confirmation before provisioning.
- First-workflow onboarding session.
- Configuration support for the selected first workflow.
- Reasonable operational monitoring appropriate to the selected tier.
- Reasonable backup posture appropriate to the selected tier and hosting stack.
- Email support through the named support contact(s).
- Export or handoff of agreed proof artifacts at pilot closeout when included in the SOW.

## Availability Posture

Baseline managed hosting is offered on a commercially reasonable availability basis for supervised planning workflows.

Unless an enhanced SLA is attached in writing:

- no financially backed uptime percentage is promised;
- no service credits are offered;
- no 24/7 incident response is included;
- maintenance windows may be scheduled with reasonable notice when practical;
- outages caused by client networks, client identity systems, third-party platforms, public data-source downtime, Mapbox/Supabase/Vercel/Stripe availability, browser/device constraints, or other events outside provider control are outside the baseline availability target.

If the client requires a formal uptime percentage, service credits, 24/7 response, private-cloud deployment, or agency-cloud deployment, attach a separate enhanced support/SLA schedule before signature.

## Backup And Restore Posture

Baseline backup and restore posture must be confirmed for each engagement before signature because it depends on the selected hosting stack, data sensitivity, and service tier.

Default buyer-safe posture:

- Provider maintains reasonable backup practices for hosted workspace data using the managed hosting stack selected for the engagement.
- Provider may also retain agreed proof exports, report artifacts, or handoff files when the SOW requires them.
- Backup availability is not a substitute for client record-retention duties, public-records duties, official plan archives, or legal hold obligations.
- Restore timing is handled as a support request unless an enhanced recovery objective is attached.

## Support Targets

Baseline support is email-based and handled during normal business support hours unless an enhanced support addendum says otherwise.

| Priority | Example | Baseline Target |
|---|---|---|
| P1 - workspace unavailable | Scoped workspace cannot be reached by authorized users during business support hours | Acknowledge within 1 business day; work toward restoration or workaround |
| P2 - workflow blocked | Selected first workflow cannot proceed because of a platform or configuration issue | Acknowledge within 2 business days |
| P3 - question or minor issue | Usage question, copy adjustment, non-blocking configuration issue | Acknowledge within 2 business days |
| Change request | New workflow, custom report, integration, data migration, SSO/private cloud, or expanded support | Scope and quote separately |

Support targets are response targets, not guaranteed resolution times, unless a stricter support schedule is attached.

## Escalation Path

1. Client support contact sends the issue to the named Nat Ford support contact with workspace, affected workflow, screenshots or steps, urgency, and desired outcome.
2. Nat Ford acknowledges according to the support target and triages whether the issue is platform, data, configuration, third-party service, or scope-related.
3. If the issue blocks the scoped first workflow, Nat Ford identifies a workaround, restoration path, or scope decision.
4. If the issue requires new work, third-party vendor action, legal/procurement review, or an enhanced support obligation, Nat Ford escalates to the client owner for written direction before expanding scope.
5. Material pilot-impacting issues are summarized in the closeout memo or service review when one is included.

## Client Responsibilities

- Name one workspace owner and at least one authorized support contact.
- Confirm who may access the workspace and which roles they should have.
- Provide accurate data, documents, project lists, local context, and review feedback.
- Classify data sensitivity before upload or configuration.
- Review AI-assisted, modeling, report, map, grant, and compliance outputs before external reliance.
- Maintain official records outside OpenPlan when required by law, policy, grant terms, or procurement rules.
- Pause use or escalation if billing, data sensitivity, or scope becomes unclear.

## Provider Responsibilities

- Operate the scoped hosted workspace consistent with this schedule and the applicable SOW.
- Keep the support path clear for the named client contact(s).
- Use buyer-safe caveats for AI, modeling, compliance, billing, and open-source license boundaries.
- Avoid describing the engagement as fully self-serve SaaS, autonomous planning, validated forecasting, legal sign-off, or complete compliance automation.
- Document material scope, support, or proof-boundary changes before relying on them externally.

## Out Of Scope Unless Added In Writing

- Legal, regulatory, engineering, procurement, or grant-submission sign-off.
- Certified or calibrated behavioral forecasting validation.
- Autonomous AI planning decisions.
- Complete LAPM or compliance automation.
- Unlimited custom software development.
- Unlimited planning services or consultant work.
- 24/7 incident response.
- Financially backed uptime SLA or service credits.
- Production SSO, private cloud, agency-cloud deployment, or custom security review.
- Fresh same-cycle paid checkout canary proof for the May 1, 2026 release-to-sale packet.

## Billing Proof Boundary

For the May 1, 2026 release-to-sale packet, OpenPlan managed-hosting billing is positioned as historical live payment evidence plus current non-money-moving billing proof. A fresh same-cycle paid checkout canary was waived and must not be described as freshly re-proven.

Paid activation for a specific workspace should be treated as a supervised workspace-specific step. If procurement requires stronger payment proof, decide before signature whether to run a fresh paid canary or attach a billing proof addendum.

## Acceptance And Review

The managed-hosting schedule is ready for a scoped pilot or service engagement when:

- the order form fields are filled;
- support contacts and workspace owner are named;
- the first workflow is scoped;
- data sensitivity is classified;
- backup/restore posture is either filled or explicitly deferred;
- enhanced SLA needs are either attached or marked out of scope;
- billing proof language matches the May 1 release-to-sale waiver;
- and client/provider reviewers confirm that legal, procurement, and records obligations are handled outside this operational schedule.
